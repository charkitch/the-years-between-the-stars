A lot of games have a sort of fallen world asthetic. The vaults are always empty.  The cities are always in ruins. The aliens are always dead.

This is not that game. The galaxy is alive and well. There are thriving civilizations, bustling stations, and active trade routes. There are also ruins, wonders, and mysteries. But the galaxy is not a graveyard. It is a vibrant, dynamic place full of life and activity.

Things are neither perfect nor the worst they’ve ever been. The game’s story takes place across a long time, and things will get better and worse in different places for different people.. 

A lot of space games have a game loop based on combat. This is a peaceful-(ish) game. 

The vibe goal is a combo of Doctor Who, the shatterlings from House of Suns and the Qeng Ho. We travel the galaxy and through time (in one way, but more quickly - from seome reference frames - than most). 

We interact, primarily through trade. We astonish. We are astonished. Occasionally, even as the Doctor does, we must intercede in ways we will get sad about. Sometimes we intercede and discover, much later, we are wrong. Sometimes we discover, much, much later, we were wrong in interesting ways. 

We often see super powerful aliens a thing of the ancient past, not so here. We will come across working civilizations vastly more capable than us, engigmatic, not falling, not shadows of their former selves.
